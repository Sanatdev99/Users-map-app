## Shipping Your First Vehicle? Here's What You Need to Know

Shipping a car for the first time can feel overwhelming. Follow these expert tips for a smooth, stress-free experience.

---

## Before You Book

### 1. Research Carriers
‚úì Check BBB accreditation
‚úì Read customer reviews
‚úì Verify DOT and MC numbers
‚úì Confirm insurance coverage

### 2. Get Multiple Quotes
Compare at least 3-5 quotes to understand market rates.

**Red Flags:**
- Prices significantly lower than competitors
- No physical address or phone number
- Requests for large upfront deposits
- Vague or unclear terms

### 3. Understand Your Quote
Make sure it includes:
- Pickup and delivery locations
- Transport type (open/enclosed)
- Insurance coverage amount
- Estimated delivery timeframe
- Payment terms

## Preparing Your Vehicle

### Exterior Preparation
1. **Wash thoroughly** - Document condition
2. **Take photos** - All angles, close-ups of existing damage
3. **Remove accessories** - Antennas, spoilers, roof racks
4. **Check tire pressure** - Properly inflated tires

### Interior Preparation
1. **Remove all personal items** - Not covered by insurance
2. **Secure loose parts** - Anything that could rattle or move
3. **Disable alarm system** - Prevent issues during transport
4. **Leave 1/4 tank of gas** - Required for loading/unloading

### Mechanical Check
‚úì No fluid leaks
‚úì Battery charged
‚úì Brakes functional
‚úì Tire condition good

## During Pickup

### What to Expect
1. **Inspection**: Driver will document vehicle condition
2. **Bill of Lading**: You'll sign this important document
3. **Keys**: Provide one set of keys
4. **Communication**: Get driver's contact information

### Important Documents
- **Bill of Lading** - Keep your copy safe
- **Insurance Certificate** - Review coverage details
- **Contact Information** - Driver and company contacts

## At Delivery

### Inspection Checklist
1. **Check exterior** - Compare to pickup photos
2. **Note any damage** - Document on Bill of Lading
3. **Test vehicle** - Ensure it runs properly
4. **Review paperwork** - Before driver leaves

### If You Find Damage
1. **Document immediately** - Photos and notes
2. **Note on Bill of Lading** - Before signing
3. **Contact carrier** - File claim within 24 hours
4. **Keep all records** - Photos, documents, communications

---

## Payment

### Common Payment Options
- Credit/Debit cards
- Cash on delivery
- Certified check
- Bank transfer

**Typical Payment Schedule:**
- Deposit: 10-20% at booking
- Balance: At delivery

**Never:**
- Pay 100% upfront
- Use untraceable methods
- Pay before seeing your vehicle

## Questions to Ask Your Carrier

1. What's your DOT/MC number?
2. What insurance coverage do you provide?
3. What's the estimated pickup and delivery window?
4. Will my car be on a dedicated or shared carrier?
5. What happens if there's a delay?
6. How do I track my shipment?
7. What's your claims process?
